# Research Memo: AI-Native Knowledge Work

Recent AI assistants suggest that productivity improves when users can rapidly summarize documents and generate drafts. However, this framing may be too narrow for complex strategy work.

The evidence indicates that decision quality depends on comparing source logic, identifying uncertainty, and seeing which assumptions drive a conclusion.

We assume that researchers, PMs, founders, and strategists already have source material but lack a durable environment for thought evolution.

On the other hand, generic automation tools conflict with the goal of deeper thinking because they hide intermediate reasoning. This can create confidence without understanding.

A useful system might therefore make contradictions visible, show alternative framings, and turn disagreement into experiments.

The weak point is that synthesis quality depends heavily on source quality and model judgment. The system should make this uncertainty explicit rather than masking it with polished prose.
